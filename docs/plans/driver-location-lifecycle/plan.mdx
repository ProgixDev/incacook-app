### Driver App — Location Lifecycle

The driver app streams the courier's GPS through one shared `LocationService`,
but two controllers drive it: `DeliveryDriverController` owns the **online**
session and `DeliveryRouteController` owns the **active delivery**. The single
stream runs in one of two platform modes — a battery-light **foreground** mode
while the driver is idle-online, and a **background** mode (Android
foreground-service + iOS background updates) that keeps the fix alive while the
app is backgrounded mid-delivery, so live tracking never goes dark.

Because two controllers share one stream, the *mode transitions* are the fragile
part. This document captures the corrected lifecycle and the two ordering bugs
just fixed: a relaunch-mid-delivery path that silently downgraded background →
foreground, and an idle-GPS stall after a completed delivery. It is the first of
a driver-app series under `docs/plans/`.

### End-to-end job flow

Once online, `IncomingOrderController` polls `GET /drivers/me/deliveries/available`,
surfaces the first routable offer in the modal, and on accept the home screen
`claim()`s it and calls `DeliveryRouteController.acceptJob()`, which bootstraps
the driver → seller → client itinerary and upgrades the stream to background.
The lifecycle then walks these stages — the persistent action bar and the full
`JobLifecycleCard` share one `runStageCta`, so the compact and expanded views can
never diverge on the money/penalty QR paths.

<Callout id="qr-note" tone="info">

Pickup and delivery are **not** best-effort advances: they are token-validated QR
scans (`confirmPickupScanned` / `confirmDeliveryScanned`) that rethrow `ApiFailure`
so an invalid or duplicate QR surfaces the backend message and the stage does not
advance. Fallbacks — seller-unavailable at pickup, client-absent (photo + GPS) at
dropoff — and realtime `delivery:cancelled` events all clear the job the same way.

</Callout>

### The two defects fixed

Both were ordering bugs around the shared stream — invisible to `flutter analyze`
and to the widget layer, which is why they survived until an end-to-end trace.

<Table
  id="defect-table"
  columns={["#", "Symptom", "Root cause", "Fix"]}
  rows={[
    ["1", "After an app restart during a delivery, background persistence was silently off — the driver could 'disappear' while backgrounded.", "restoreActiveJob() starts the stream in background, then restoreOnlineState() calls start() (foreground); the old guard restarted the stream, tearing the foreground-service down.", "start() only restarts on an upgrade; a foreground start() over a live background stream is a no-op (shouldRestartStream)."],
    ["2", "After finishing a delivery while staying online, GPS stopped entirely — idle matching pushed the frozen drop-off point and the online marker never moved.", "clearJob() calls stop() unconditionally; nothing re-armed the stream while the driver was still online.", "_onJobChanged re-arms foreground GPS on clear-while-online; stop() resets its mode flags synchronously so the re-arm doesn't race the in-flight cancel."],
  ]}
/>
